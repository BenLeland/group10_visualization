When Everything Falls Apart, Where Does Money Go?

When COVID-19 was declared a pandemic in March 2020, Bitcoin crashed 50% in 48 hours. The S&P 500 fell 30%. Oil prices eventually went negative. But Gold and the US Dollar? They grew stronger. This wasn't random—it was a pattern that repeats across crises.

Over 12 years (2013-2025), we've witnessed three major boom-bust cycles and countless global shocks. Each time uncertainty strikes—whether pandemic, war, or financial collapse—assets diverge in predictable ways. Safe havens like Gold and USD typically strengthen as investors flee risk. Meanwhile, volatile assets like Bitcoin and stocks plummet, only to surge during recovery periods.

This visualization lets you explore these patterns yourself. Drag the timeline to watch how different assets responded to the same events. The size of each bubble shows its relative performance—bigger means stronger, smaller means weaker. Notice how Bitcoin swings wildly between massive and microscopic, while Gold barely budges. See how USD grows during every crisis, then shrinks during every boom. The patterns aren't guarantees, but they reveal something fundamental: different assets serve different purposes. Some amplify gains and losses. Others provide shelter during storms.
